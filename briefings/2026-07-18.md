### OpenAI trains an AI model to attack its own models
**What it is:** OpenAI built an internal model, GPT-Red, whose sole job is finding exploitable weaknesses in OpenAI's other models before release — reportedly reaching an 84% attack success rate on novel safety scenarios versus 13% for human red-teamers, and cutting a previously reliable "fake chain-of-thought" jailbreak from over 95% success on GPT-5.1 to under 10% on GPT-5.6 Sol.

**The principle:** A static red-team benchmark goes stale the moment a model is patched against the exact attacks already in the test set. Training an attacker model and a defender model together through self-play reinforcement learning means each side keeps adapting to the other's latest move, so hardening never stops chasing a fixed, memorizable list of known exploits.

**Why it matters:** The reported six-fold drop in jailbreak success without a matching rise in refusals on legitimate requests suggests automated adversarial training can close real safety gaps without the usual cost of models becoming needlessly more restrictive.

Source: https://openai.com/index/unlocking-self-improvement-gpt-red/

### Google renames NotebookLM to Gemini Notebook, adds code execution
**What it is:** Google renamed NotebookLM — its source-grounded research tool used by more than 30 million people — to Gemini Notebook, and added a sandboxed "secure cloud computer" that can execute code directly against a user's uploaded sources, rather than only summarizing and citing them.

**The principle:** A tool that only summarizes or quotes source material is limited to restating what's already written in the documents. Giving that same tool a code-execution sandbox lets it derive new numbers, tables, or transformations from the source data instead — the difference between reporting what a spreadsheet says and actually running a calculation on it.

**Why it matters:** It folds a code-interpreter-style analysis capability into a product built around trustworthy citations, testing whether execution and grounded summarization can coexist without the tool hallucinating results the way general-purpose code interpreters sometimes do.

Source: https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/products/gemini-notebook/notebooklm-gemini-notebook/

## Science

### First atmosphere detected around a temperate rocky exoplanet
**What it is:** Astronomers used the WINERED spectrograph on the Magellan Clay telescope to watch LHS 1140 b — a rocky, potentially habitable-zone planet orbiting a nearby red dwarf — transit its star, and detected helium being stripped from its upper atmosphere during 2024 observations; the same signal was absent in 2025.

**The principle:** Helium is light enough that stellar radiation should blow it off a planet's atmosphere and disperse it quickly unless it's constantly replenished from below. Detecting escaping helium in one year and not the next implies a layered atmosphere — a helium-rich upper layer sitting above heavier gases like water vapor closer to the surface — with the escape rate itself tracking the star's own activity cycle.

**Why it matters:** It's the first direct evidence that a temperate rocky planet around a red dwarf — the most common star type in the galaxy, and notoriously hostile to atmospheres — can actually hold onto one, at least in part.

Source: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.aea9708

### AI designs a gene-editing enzyme that beats natural evolution
**What it is:** Jennifer Doudna's lab at the Innovative Genomics Institute used an AI protein-design pipeline to generate new gene-editing enzymes modeled on the compact TnpB/Cas12 family, then tested them in bacterial, human, and plant cells; two AI-designed variants edited human genes at 46% and 50% efficiency, versus 28% for the natural enzyme.

**The principle:** A natural gene-editing enzyme is shaped by whatever mutations happened to survive billions of years of evolution, not by what edits DNA most efficiently. An inverse protein-folding model can instead generate a wholly new amino acid sequence engineered to fold into the same functional scaffold, and cryo-EM shows the AI-made versions form new stabilizing contacts at the RNA-DNA interface that the natural enzyme never evolved.

**Why it matters:** It's direct evidence that AI protein design can outperform evolution's solution for a specific engineering target, pointing toward custom-built gene-editing tools rather than ones limited to whatever variants nature happens to supply.

Source: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.aed6123

### On-skin sensors invisible to sight yet beat gel electrodes
**What it is:** A University of Tokyo-led team built ultrathin, roughly 200-nanometer on-skin electrodes for recording brain, eye-movement, and muscle activity that are effectively invisible and untouchable on the skin, yet register lower skin-electrode impedance — meaning cleaner signal — than standard gel electrodes.

**The principle:** Conventional electrodes are visible mainly because their glossy surface reflects light differently than skin does, not because of their electrical properties. Matching a conductive nanowire film's roughness and reflectance to real skin removes that visual artifact entirely, decoupling how detectable a sensor looks from how well it conducts — the two properties turn out not to trade off against each other at all.

**Why it matters:** Visible sensors are known to make wearers self-conscious, which can itself distort the brain, muscle, and eye signals being measured; a sensor nobody can see or feel removes that confound for long-duration monitoring.

Source: https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.aee6417
